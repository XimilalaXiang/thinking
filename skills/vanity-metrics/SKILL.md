# Vanity Metrics: Pick Better Goals

A thinking skill based on [Julian Shapiro's framework](https://www.julian.com/blog/vanity-metrics) for identifying vanity metrics and redirecting focus toward goals that actually matter.

## Core Principle

Vanity metrics are:
1. **Intermediary goals** that occur before the fulfilling goals that matter
2. **Easier to accomplish** than your true goals
3. **Frequently encouraged by society** (likes, followers, credentials)

## When to Apply This Skill

Trigger this analysis whenever someone is:
- Setting personal or professional goals
- Evaluating KPIs or success metrics for a project/startup
- Planning what to work on next
- Questioning whether they're focused on the right things
- Reviewing career milestones or accomplishments
- Designing a growth strategy (product, audience, personal brand)

## The Three Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Goal Path

Use when the goal involves **creating change or achieving an outcome**.

**How it works:** Map the steps from input to ultimate goal as a linear path.

```
[Time/Resources] → [Step 1] → [Step 2] → ... → [Ultimate Goal]
```

**Rules:**
- Steps near the **beginning** are most likely vanity metrics
- **Time and resources are always vanity metrics** — they're the very first step
- Only obsess over an early step if it's a **bottleneck** preventing the next step
- If a step is NOT a bottleneck, 80/20 it and move on

**Example — Building a Newsletter:**
```
Website visits → Subscribes → Opens → Reads → Clicks & takes action
     ^                                              ^
  vanity metric                               ultimate goal
```

**Example — Finance:**
```
Possessions → Income → Net worth
     ^                     ^
  vanity                 real
```

**Example — Work:**
```
Employee headcount → Revenue growth → Profit growth
       ^                                    ^
    vanity                                real
```

**Application template:**
1. Ask: "What is the ultimate goal?"
2. Map all steps backward from that goal
3. Identify which step the person is optimizing
4. If they're stuck on an early step that isn't a bottleneck, redirect them

### Framework 2: The Ability Spectrum

Use when the goal involves **signaling ability or credibility**.

**How it works:** Place accomplishments on a spectrum from easy-to-game to hard-to-game.

```
Easy to Game ◄──────────────────────► Hard to Game
(vanity)                               (authentic)
```

**Key insight:** Hard-to-game accomplishments share one trait — they **create valuable output**.

| Signal | Why it's Gameable | Better Alternative |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Forbes 30 Under 30 | Lobbying, politics, adverse selection | Data-backed rankings (e.g., best employee-reviewed CEO) |
| Harvard MBA | Time + money signal, exam engineering | Revenue impact of companies you've founded/consulted |
| 1M followers | Can be bought, bots, trend-following | Engagement rate and ability to influence action |
| GitHub stars | Can be botted, one-click effort | Actual adoption, production usage, contributor count |

**Application template:**
1. Ask: "What ability are you trying to signal?"
2. Evaluate how gameable the current signal is
3. Propose a harder-to-game alternative that creates output
4. Remind: "Let your output be your signal, not your credentials"

### Framework 3: The Virtue Spectrum

Use when the goal involves **signaling values or making an impact**.

**How it works:** Place actions on a spectrum from low-effort to high-effort.

```
Low Effort ◄──────────────────────► High Effort
(virtue signaling)                    (authentic impact)
```

**Key insight:** Authenticity is measured by how much **meaningful change you attempt to produce**.

**Example — Environmental concern:**
```
Complaining on Twitter → Tweeting a politician → Creating a volunteer group → Organizing a march
        ^                                                                           ^
    low effort / vanity                                                   high effort / real
```

**Application template:**
1. Ask: "What virtue or cause are you trying to advance?"
2. Identify where the current action falls on the effort spectrum
3. Propose a higher-effort action that produces real change
4. Warn: "Low-effort actions can mislead you into thinking you're having impact"

## How to Use: The Quick Check

When evaluating any goal or metric, run these three questions:

1. **Am I signaling a virtue?** → Focus on behaviors that have impact. Otherwise, you trade long-term change for short-term validation.
2. **Am I signaling an ability?** → Focus on value creation that is hard to game. Otherwise, your work won't impress talented people.
3. **Am I chasing an outcome?** → Don't obsess over early steps in your goal path. Otherwise, you may never reach your ultimate goal.
